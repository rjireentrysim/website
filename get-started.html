Get Started

A Reentry Simulation can be a powerful tool to bring communities together to support their formerly-incarcerated neighbors. We've seen groups use what they learned to plan actionable steps to dismantle barriers to transportation, employment, childcare, and more. Planning and facilitating a simulation can be a heavy lift — we've put together these resources to make it as light as possible.

01
6–8 Months Before Your Event
Deciding who should attend
+

6–8 months out from your event, conceptualize its purpose. Consider who in your community has the power to make change. Think about inviting:

People who interface with people who are reentering
  • Social workers
  • Treatment providers
  • Law enforcement
  • University administration
  • Judges & lawyers
People whose decisions make an impact
  • County government
  • Transportation administrators
  • Representatives
  • Housing boards

We highly recommend inviting justice-impacted members of your community to participate from behind the tables rather than in the role of reentrants (see "Recruiting Volunteers").

Some audiences may already recognize that there's a problem with our system, but others may not. It's best to have a balance of both.
02
6 Months Before Your Event
Finding partnerships
+

Think strategically. If possible, try to match the mission of your chosen partners to the values of your target audience. 6 months from your event, reach out to local groups involved with reentry, higher education in prison, housing advocacy, justice reform, or professional development for social workers. While they likely can't be financial supporters, you may also find help from churches, community centers, shelters, or food banks.

Reentry Simulations can be run for free, but partners can help provide:

  • A large space to host
  • Volunteers
  • Marketing / visibility
  • Printed materials
  • Facilitators
  • Panelists (optional)
  • Food for participants (optional)

When you contact partners, it's important to:

  • Explain what a reentry simulation is and why it's important
  • Explain what role you'd like them to play and why
  • Be upfront with your needs (see list above)
03
4 Months Before Your Event
Planning the logistics
+

Length: We recommend booking a space for 3–4 hours. The components are:

  • 30 minutes for set up
  • 30 minutes for volunteer training (can be done on Zoom prior)
  • 90–120 minutes for the simulation
  • 1 hour for a panel or community planning session (optional)

Date and time: Weekend mornings or afternoons work best. Weekdays may work if you have a guaranteed group of participants who are available (e.g. students in a class, teachers doing professional development). Weeknights are challenging.

Space: Find a large open space you can book like a school gym, community center, or college campus recreational space. Partners may be able to help. See "Setting up the room" for the recommended floor plan. You should have 7–12 folding tables set up as stations around the perimeter.

Avoid auditoriums with immovable seats.
04
3 Months Before Your Event
Save the Date
+

3 months from your event, send out a Save the Date. An email with the event name, time, date, location, and RSVP Google Form does the trick. Ask partners to forward this to organizations, listservs, and anyone else who might be interested.

Use the Google Form to ask people if they're interested in volunteering. Explain that it will only take an extra 30 minutes of training.

Reentry Simulation

RSVP here

Come navigate systemic obstacles encountered by justice impacted individuals returning home such as employment, housing, and education. Facilitators will then lead discussions about the experience and how to make actionable change.

Event is open to community members and organizations*, and food will be provided!

[Date and time]
[ADDRESS]

Click here to RSVP.

This interactive session is organized by [CO-SPONSORS]

*NOTE: If you have lived experience of incarceration and reentry, you may want to join as a volunteer rather than as a participant. The experience of the simulation is geared toward people who are just learning about the system, and your expertise will be particularly valuable on the other side of the table. You will also have the opportunity to join the volunteers the day of and will not be asked to disclose any information.

05
2–3 Months Before Your Event
Designing flyers
+
✓ Do
  • Include the event title, date, place, and RSVP method prominently
  • Include accessibility info, contact information, and co-sponsor logos
  • Include a way to sign up to volunteer
✗ Don't
  • Use language like "take a walk in their shoes" — we're simulating a system, not a person's full lived experience
  • Use images of one person alone — we don't want to suggest that any one person can represent reentry
Try imagery that symbolizes systemic barriers like a maze, a long line, or a stack of paperwork.
06
2 Months Before Your Event
Marketing the simulation
+

2 months from your event, circulate your flyer. Remember to use a form for RSVPs and volunteer interest. Even if they're not official partners, ask community organizations, professional groups, and educational organizations to circulate your flyer.

Try reaching out to elected officials and county governments. Even if they can't come, many will send a staff member.

07
6 Weeks Before Your Event
Preparing materials
+

6 weeks from your event, prepare your physical materials. You'll need:

  • Hanging name tags (1 per participant plus a few extra)*
  • Clear binder sleeves (1 per participant plus 1 per station)*
  • Pens (2 per station)
  • Sticky note pad (1 per station)
  • Manila envelopes (1 per station)
  • Clipboards (1 per facilitator)
  • Painters tape, rubber bands, scissors, binder clips, and other office supplies
  • Printouts: instructions, profiles, money, transportation tickets, etc. — all files are under the "Access Materials" tab
*RJI may be able to provide pre-made boxes with these items. Please reach out at the contact link below if you're interested!
Cutting out and organizing materials can be MUCH more time-consuming than you might expect. Rally help and leave yourself time!
08
5 Weeks Before Your Event
Selecting an add-on
+

5 weeks from your event, select 1–2 add-on profiles based on your audience.

Because identity is key to how people experience incarceration and reentry, we developed profiles to represent challenges related to gender, childcare, ageing, digital literacy, domestic violence, and higher education (all under "Profiles"). We recommend selecting two of these add-ons based on your audience and the needs of your local community.

09
1 Month Before Your Event
Recruiting volunteers
+

1 month from your event, identify 25 volunteers who are willing to come half an hour early to train. Instead of participating as reentrants, they will work behind the tables and simulate offices like social services, employers, and more. They do not need to have experience with simulations.

Use this spreadsheet to track volunteers. The stations with higher numbers have higher difficulty; we recommend doubling up volunteers here.

You can run the simulation with as few as 12 volunteers if necessary. We've found that 25 gives you room for no-shows, check-in desk volunteers, and doubling up at the more difficult tables.

Lean on your partner organizations to find volunteers rather than simply asking the public.

10
3 Weeks Before Your Event
Organizing your materials
+

Participant Profiles: In the nametag holders, include:

  • Name
  • Money (amount based on profile)
  • Transportation tickets (amount based on profile)
  • Pawn items (amount based on profile)

Then, put these in the clear binder sleeves along with a participant profile and the matching life card.

Stations: Each station has its own instructions for what to include. Assign one manila envelope per station, then fill that envelope with the instruction page, a sign for the station (folded cardstock works!), and suggested materials.

Cutting out and organizing materials can be MUCH more time-consuming than you might expect. Rally help and leave yourself time!
11
1–2 Weeks Before Your Event
Send reminders
+

1–2 weeks from your event, send reminders to participants, volunteers, and partners. Be sure to include:

  • Arrival time expectations (especially for volunteers)
  • A reminder that we encourage formerly-incarcerated participants to join behind the stations rather than as reentrants
12
The Morning of Your Event
Setting up the room
+

The morning of your event, set up the room using the floor plan below. You can combine 2 stations per table depending on the size of your space. Place the station envelopes on the tables. The tables do not have to match the floor plan exactly.

📐 Floor Plan PDF
For traffic flow, we recommend 1) a check-in table where participants receive profiles and 2) not doubling up the ID table (which gets crowded early on).
