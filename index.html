What is a reentry simulation?

A reentry simulation is a 1.5–2 hour experience where participants are given the identity of a fictional person returning home from prison. The profiles represent different challenges across gender, childcare, ageing, digital literacy, domestic violence, higher education, and more.

They navigate simulated barriers like housing, transportation, childcare, and more, noticing along the way their own feelings of frustration, anger, solidarity, and occasional joy. Facilitators lead debrief sessions throughout.

Take a look at an example of a simulation by the Penn State team:

We don't believe that a simulation can — or should — capture the full, complex experience of returning home from prison. It is not a way to "walk in someone's shoes," but it is a way to illustrate systemic barriers.
